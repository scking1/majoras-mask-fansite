Development

Even though Majora's Mask was the second instalment in the 3D Legend of Zelda game catalogue, the development process was vastly different from its predecessor, Ocarina of Time. The development team was led by Eiji Aonuma, Yoshiaki Koizumi, and Shigeru Miyamoto. Both Miyamoto and Aonuma worked on Ocarina of Time. While Ocarina of Time took 5 years to develop, Miyamoto challenged the team working on Majora's Mask to complete the game within a year. The game engine and many of the assets were reused from Ocarina of Time which allowed the team to develop the entire game within 15 months.

Technical Differences from Ocarina of Time

The game was initially planned to be a remixed edition of Ocarina of Time released for the Nintendo 64DD (Disk Drive) peripheral. However, due to the commercial failure of the 64DD in Japan, the 64DD never got an international release. Majora's Mask was released for the N64 but because it used an upgraded version of the Ocarina of Time engine, it required the Nintendo 64's 4MB Expansion Pak to be playable. The expansion pack allowed for greater draw distance which eliminated the need for the fog effect and cardboard panorama which were used in Ocarina of Time to obscure distant areas. It also allowed for more characters to be on screen at once.
