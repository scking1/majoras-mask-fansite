Music

The music for The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask was written by Koji Kondo with contributions from Toru Minegishi. Koji Kondo is best known for his many musical contributions to Nintendo's Super Mario and The Legend of Zelda series. Kondo has said that the music of Majora's Mask resembles that of a Chinese opera. The soundtrack consists of reworked tracks from Ocarina of Time, some familiar music from previous The Legend of Zelda games, and new material.

Music as Part of Gameplay

Like Ocarina of Time, music is part of the gameplay in Majora's Mask. By playing certain songs on his Ocarina Link is able do things like travel through time, alter the passing of time, and heal those that have been affected by Majora's curse. Using special masks, Link is also able to transform into three of the different species that inhabit Termina: a Deku Scrub, a Goran, and a Zora. When Link transforms into one of the different species, his Ocarina transforms into a different musical instrument. In Deku form, Link's Ocarina becomes a set of brass pipes known as Deku pipes. In Goran form, his Ocarina becomes a set of drums and when Link transforms into a Zora, his Ocarina turns into a fish bone guitar. To complete certain parts of the game, Link must transform and play a particular song on one of the three different instruments.
