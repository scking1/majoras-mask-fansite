Remake for the 3DS

After the 2011 release of Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time 3D, a remake for the Nintendo 3DS handheld console, development began on a remake of Majora's Mask. The development team consisted of The Legend of Zelda series producer Eiji Aonuma and co-developers Grezzo, a Japanese video game development company who also worked on Ocarina of Time 3D. The project was kept under wraps during development. Both Aonuma and the team at Grezzo came up with a list of moments in the original N64 version that seemed unreasonable for players that they called the “what in the world” list. The team adjusted each item on the list in the remake and as a result, Aonuma felt that the 3DS remake feels more forgiving than original version.

Differences from the Original N64 Version

Like it's predecessor, Ocarina of Time 3D, Majors Mask 3D is an enhanced remake that features stereoscopic 3D graphics, more detailed environmental textures, a higher framerate of 30 frames-per-second, a new touchscreen user interface, and gyroscopic aiming.

There were several features of the game that were updated for the 3DS remake. One of the upgraded features was the Song of Double Time. In the original N64 version, the Song of Double Time allowed the player to travel forward in time but only in 6 hour intervals. In Majora's Mask 3D, the player can travel forward to any hour of the given day. The Bomber's Notebook was an inventory item that players could use to log information about select non-playable characters (NPCs). It was enhanced in the remake to include detailed information about all of the NPCs.
